Research Interests

Visual and Haptic perception

Perception is an active process: I'm interested in how things appear, depending on their physical properties and they way we sample them with our senses.

My long-term goal is to provide the missing link between saliency and perceptual appearance and an understanding of how information is integrated across spatial locations when sampled at different visual eccentricities. To test for generality, I want to export my findings from vision totouch, looking at how information is selected and integrated across with different fingers.

Bio

Matteo Toscani

Since 2021 I'm a Lecturer at Bournemouth University. I did my undergraduate work in Florence (Italy), followed by an MSc thesis on the electroencephalographiccorrelates of visual sensitivity and memory for facial emotions. Ever since, one of my main goals was totackle the link between physiology and behaviour. After my graduation I moved to Giessen (Germany) to work in one of Europe’s top vision laboratories. There I developed interest in visual appearance of complex realistic stimuli. I worked on color with Karl Gegenfurtner,and lately onthe perception of material properties with Roland Fleming and Katja Dörschner. Outside Giessen, I worked with Pascal Mamassian investigating metacognition in peripheral vision, and appearance of complex materials with Claudio and Dar’ya Guarnera. By working with Arash Akbarinia I was introduced to the vast potential of DNNs. I am working on touch perception in collaboration with the haptics group of Knut Drewing in Giessen and with my wife Anna Metzger, as I am convinced that the search for common principles of selective sampling and salient features in haptics and vision wil llead to major insights in both fields.

My approach combines eye movement recordings with computer graphics and machine learning. Graphics allows to produce a large number of simulated scenes with a known ground truth (e.g.the surface reflection parameters used for rendering). I use machine learning to determine which image properties of the simulated scenes are particularly diagnostic for their physical ground truth.
